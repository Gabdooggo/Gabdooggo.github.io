Brazillian Pizza

Jump to Recipe

Background

Pizza arrived in Brazil through Italian immigrants who came to Brazil between 1880 and 1930, which is also about the same time they came to the U.S. Before the 1950's, pizza was eaten almost exclusively by Brazilians of italian descent, however since then its become more widely known, and quickly became a national favorite. One of the main things that make Brazillian pizza differ from american is the crust. You can find a variety of crusts in the U.S. However Brazil always has very thin crust, and the edge of it is usually filled with cheddar or another cheese called Requeijao. Apart from the crust Brazillian pizza's differ from others as they barely use any tomato sauce.

Tips and tricks

  • Make sure to coat the baking sheet with a bit of oil so the pizza doesn't get stuck on it.

  • For the pizza sauce you can use ketchup

  • Some ingredients that can make your homemade pizza more flavorful are olive oil, and flour. So if you want to make your pizza more flavorful use one of those.

Prep Time

The prep time takes about 5 minutes. The cooktime takes approximately 10 minutes. The total time is about 15 minutes.

Servings

There is about 8 servings in this recipe

Instructions

Ingredients for Brazillian Pizza
Amount Ingredient
1 Thin Crust
1/2 cup Chunky Pizza Sauce
2 cups Shredded Mozzarella cheese
1/3 cup Sliced Olives
1/2 Thinly Sliced Onion
2/3 cup Diced Ham
2 sliced Hard Boiled Eggs
Step 1

First you should preheat the oven to 425 degrees F, then place the pizza crust on the baking sheet.

Step 2

Spread pizza sauce onto the curst, and top it with 1/2 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese.

Step 3

Add olives, onion slices, ham, and hard-boiled eggs. Then top it all of with the remaining cheese.

Step 4

Bake for 8-10 minutes or up until the cheese is melted.

Step 5

Take out of the oven and slice into even pieces,(or not, you choose) and enjoy your new homemade Brazillian Pizza!
